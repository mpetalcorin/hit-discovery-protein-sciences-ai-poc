# DataSheet
This DataSheet documents the synthetic datasets generated in ` Hit Discovery Protein Sciences.ipynb`. The purpose is to demonstrate discovery analytics and modelling workflows with realistic data shapes and failure modes (noise, drift, missingness), while avoiding proprietary or sensitive data.
## 1. Motivation
**Why these datasets exist**
- To demonstrate HTS QC and hit calling.
- To demonstrate integrating heterogeneous sources (CSV + SQL + graph).
- To demonstrate modelling on unstructured strings and sequences plus structured metadata.
- To demonstrate omics-style missingness and differential analysis.

**Intended users**
- students and scientists
## 2. Dataset Inventory

### A. HTS plate-level dataset
**File:** `az_poc_notebook/data/raw/primary_screen_plate_level.csv`

**Unit of observation:** a well on a plate  
**Columns (typical):**
- `plate`, `well_index`, `row`, `col`
- `type` (pos, neg, sample)
- `signal_raw` (synthetic assay signal)
- `pct_inhibition` (normalised to per-plate controls)
- `robust_z` (plate-wise robust z-score)
- `primary_hit` (hit call on robust_z threshold)

**Generation notes**
- Includes plate drift and realistic CV noise.
- Uses control separation consistent with robust assays.

**Primary risks simulated**
- Plate-to-plate variability
- Distribution tails (rare hits)
- Plate artefacts visible in heatmaps

### B. HTS plate QC summary
**File:** `az_poc_notebook/data/processed/hts_plate_qc.csv`  
**Unit of observation:** plate  
**Columns:**
- `Zprime`, control means, signal-to-background proxy

**Use**
- Gate assay quality prior to hit calling.

### C. Compound metadata and SAR dataset
**File:** `az_poc_notebook/data/processed/simulated_sar_compounds.csv`

**Unit of observation:** compound  
**Columns (typical):**
- `compound_id`
- `smiles` (SMILES-like placeholder string)
- `mw`, `cLogP`, `tpsa`
- `vendor`, `library`
- `motif_score`, `latent`, `p_active`, `active`

**Important**
- `smiles` is **not chemically validated**, it is used to demonstrate unstructured modelling patterns.

### D. Peptide binding dataset
**File:** `az_poc_notebook/data/processed/simulated_peptide_binding.csv`

**Unit of observation:** peptide  
**Columns (typical):**
- `peptide`, `len`
- engineered features (hydrophobicity proxy sum, charge proxy sum, aromatic count, motif indicator)
- `Kd_nM`, `pKd`

**Use**
- Demonstrate protein sciences modelling and prioritisation.

### E. LFQ proteomics raw and DE results
**Files:**
- `az_poc_notebook/data/raw/simulated_lfq_raw.csv`
- `az_poc_notebook/data/processed/simulated_lfq_de_results.csv`

**Unit of observation:**
- Raw: protein ? sample intensity matrix with missing values
- DE results: protein-level differential analysis summary

**Columns**
- Raw: `protein`, `ctrl_1..ctrl_3`, `trt_1..trt_3` (log10-scale intensities with NaNs)
- DE: `protein`, `logFC`, `pvalue`, `FDR`

**Generation notes**
- Missingness increases for lower-intensity proteins to mimic typical LFQ behaviour.

### F. SQL database
**File:** `az_poc_notebook/data/processed/az_poc.sqlite`

**Tables**
- `compounds`
- `primary_screen`

**Use**
- Demonstrate query-based analytics, aggregation, and integration.

## 3. Data Collection Process

No data was collected from experiments. All datasets are generated synthetically in the notebook, using fixed random seeds, with design choices aligned to common discovery data characteristics.

## 4. Cleaning, Preprocessing, and Labelling

### HTS
- Normalisation to `% inhibition` using per-plate positive and negative controls.
- Hit calling using robust z-score threshold.

### SAR
- Activity labels are generated from a latent score based on motifs and property windows plus noise.

### Peptides
- Binding values derived from latent energy rules plus noise, converted to Kd and pKd.

### Proteomics
- Missingness injected as a function of intensity.
- Simple imputation used for readability.

## 5. Uses and Misuses

**Appropriate uses**
- Demonstrate end-to-end workflow design and modelling patterns.
- Demonstrate QC logic, ranking, and decision reporting.

**Inappropriate uses**
- Any real compound selection, peptide design, or biological conclusion.
- Any clinical or safety decision-making.
